# Underdog Founders
<Date> Jan 05, 2026 </Date>

All founders are underdogs, but these are the kind of people who make you think you can do anything because of how disproportionately disadvantaged they were when starting out.

I like to keep a list of a few of them.

- {<ParaLink link="Jake Loosararian" href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/jake-loosararian-23981350/" />}<sup>1</sup>

- {<ParaLink link="Khalid Meniri" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_jEnJYU3e7k" />}<sup>2</sup>

- {<ParaLink link="Hamdi Ulukaya" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SGTMSV8QUrs" />}<sup>3</sup>

- {<ParaLink link="Paul Gross" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ENG_DQF5E60" />}<sup>4</sup>

<Footer>

<sup>1</sup> Started Gecko Robotics even after his family and professors told him not to do it, worked 100 hour work weeks and bootstrapped the company.

<sup>2</sup> Came to the US from Algeria with a limited education, zero background, and no connections. Learnt design by going to the Apple Store and started Selfbook down the road.

<sup>3</sup> Immigrated to the US with little money to study English, started a small cheese business and went on to start Chobani.

<sup>4</sup> Studied Computer Science at Yale but went on to found a hard tech company after reading a research paper on mobile carbon capture by his future co-founder.
